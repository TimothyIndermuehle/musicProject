8200 feet

The Kaibab Plateau is a 1,152 square mile plateau located in Arizona between Grand Canyon National Park and the Utah boarder. Kaibab is a Paiute Indian word meaning "mountain lying down", and with the Arizona Strip wilderness area to the west and the Navjo Nation to to the east making, it is one of the least populated regions in the lower 48. While the surounding area is part of the Colorado Plateau at 3500 feet above sea level, the Kaibab is located between 8000 and 9200 feet with a more temperate climate with temperatures rarely topping out above 85 degress. The yearly North American monsoon event in July and August brings afternoon thunderstorms everyday and the resulting afternoon rainbows. It is a beautiful ponderosa pine & aspen forest filled with deer and Kaibab squirrels. The highest point on the plateau is the tenth highest peak in Arizona and captures considerable snow some years.

The Grand Staircase can be seen from the Le Fevre Overlook on US highway 89A which is the north boundry of the Kaibab Plateau. It begins at Cedar Breaks Park (12,000 feet) and Bryce Canyon National Park. (9,100 feet) and is a series of plateaus and sandstone/ shale cliffs that are all multi-colored and named after the surronding rock colors. The Vermillion Cliffs is the last of the Grand Staircase steps you drive at the base on US Highway 89A before you drive up the East Kaibab Monocline, a fold in the earth, to the Kaibab Plateau. The Kaibab Plateau and Grand Canyon National Park are the southern most features of the Grand Staircase, and this all part of the Colorado Plateau. The entire region was one of the last mappped parts of the United States and is linked by a series of national and state parks. The Grand Staircase is part of the Colorado Plateau, one of the four American dessert regions in the four corners of Utah, Colorado, New Mexico and Arizona. It is a colorful desert filled with red and magenta canyons with snow capped mountains.

My Brother Esau

My Brother Essau is a 1987 single that was a B-side song to the 45 rpm record Touch of Grey. Before MP3 music and compact disks, music was played in bars on jukeboxes on 45rpm records at a cost of a quarter for three songs. It is a classic Bob Weir spacey strange song that references brothers Esau and Jacob in the book of Genesis, and the relationship with their father. The warrior brother is held in revernece by their father until he comes back a failure when America loses its first war in Vietnam.

The line "Yet sometimes at night I dream, he's still that hairy man. Shadowboxing the apocalypse, wandering the land" resonates with me for some reason.

Suzanne Vega

Tom's Diner
"Tom's Diner" is a acapela song written in 1982 by American singer and songwriter Suzanne Vega. It was first released on a studio album in 1987 and then became a bootleg cult classic. Vega discoverd the pirate copies and, instead of pressing charges, put them on a compilation albom called Tom's Album. The songs range from hip-hop to EDM to pure lounge music.

Because the song was used by German sound engineers to develop the compression format that would becoome MP3, Suzanne Vega earned the title "The Mother of the MP3"

one day in time

Eagle's Nest Ridge, Vail Mountain

Winter '91

This photo was taken the winter of 1991 with my roommates Scott and Colleen on the top of Chair 2 on Vail Mountain. Scott had the best butt on Vail Mountain and Colleen loves Milli Vanilli. I can tell that it was 1991 becase I was on alpine skis and I hadn't started growing my pony tail yet. The winds of change were blowing, however, and my life was going to be headed toward Northern Arizona in the near future and there was going to be no going back.

In the winters I worked for a Vail Ski Rentals as a ski shop assistant manager at the base of Vail Mountain, and in the summers I worked for a raft company called Raftmeister with a bunch of groovy people that I am still friends with and still raft with. I was reading everything I could get my hands on by author Edward Abbey, a naturalist and fiction writer who lived on the Colorado Plateau and fought to protect it until the day he died. His most famous work of fiction, The Monkey Wrench Gang is the story of river runners and the Grand Canyon, and I aways saw myself as the character Seldom seen Smith, the river guide. Edward Abbey was a chauvinist and highly controversial because he was one of the fathers of eco-terrorism but also wrote BEAUTIFUL naturalist writing in the tradition of Aldo Leopold and John Muir.

I had been guiding rafts for three years and had experienced a limited amount of high water during run-off and had only heard aboout the big water years in 1983 and 1984 when Glen Canyon Dam was almost destroyed. I had dreamed of seeing a historic snow year and seeing historic high water.

I've heard of an acient Chinese curse that goes something like "May you get everything you ever wished for." There was a historic snow year waiting for me to find it but I will fill you in on that one later my fabulous darlings . . . . . . . . .

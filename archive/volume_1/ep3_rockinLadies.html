top pick #3

one day in time

Suzanne Vega & the Dead

September 24, 1988
Madison Square Garden

This is a true gem. I was looking to find a link to the Deadicated album on LivexLive, a Grateful Dead tribute performed by various artists. The compilation is a rainforest benefit album, with significant proceeds from its sale being donated to the Rainforest Action Network and Cultural Survival. Suzannne Vega has a great cover of Cassidy that was written by Bob Weir and first recorded on his solo project Ace.

Suzanne Vega and her band performed earlier in the evening prior to the Dead's performance. At intermission Jerry Garcia approached Suzanne Vega about performing at the beginning of second set, and I remember reading Suzanne Vega's interview in Rolling Stone about how nervous she felt at the time. According to Vega, Jerry was kind and invited her to perform with the band. People performing with the Dead was virtually unheard of because it is hard for other musicians to mesh with their rythm and not-common cord progressions. I saw Jerry unplug Steve Miller's guitar in the middle of The Other One when Steve Miller was being a dork in Las Vegas in 1992 as a testiment to what I just said.

The YouTube video shows complete and total musical chaos as the performers try to figure each other out and what the hell is going on. (The video is a hand held audience shot so please excuse the poor quality.) The first attempt at Chinese Bones is a complete disaster but Vega turns to the drummers, re-groups, and takes charge of the song. Slowly they all pull it together; you can see Weir and Garcia watching Vega's right hand and the cords she is playing. At about 2 minutes 50 seconds of the video someting unthinkable happens! Gerry is actually DANCING ALONG with the song, something I rarely saw in the thirty plus times I saw the Dead. Usually the only movement from Jerry was him scratching himself or smoking a cigarette at which time the ausience would erupt in auplause for no apparent reason. Halfway through Suzanne Vega's song Neighborhood Girls they sound like a well oiled machine and it begins to sound like the normal Dead we we're used to hearing. Jerry Yells "I love neighborhood girls" in the background as she introduces the song. How Suzanne Vega takes charge of someone elses is band pays testiment to her musical ability, and like I said, this is a true musical gem.
